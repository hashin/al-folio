What I read today - E5

June 13, 2021

Suggestions

  1. Why Zebras Don't Get Ulcers: The Acclaimed Guide to Stress, Stress-Related Diseases, and Coping by Robert M. Sapolsky- an anthropological take on how stress affects us physiologically.
  2. The Trouble With Testosterone: And Other Essays On The Biology Of The Human Predicament by Robert M. Sapolsky
  3. A Billion Wicked Thoughts: What the Internet Tells Us About Sexual Relationships by Ogi Ogas- A book in which internet search history was analysed to make approximations about human sexuality. I don't concur with most of the findings and still think that the sample space used is too little. But a good read nevertheless.
  4. Nudge: Improving Decisions About Health, Wealth and Happiness by Richard H Thaler
  5. Train Your Brain: Ultimate Memory Hacks by Shireen Stephen
  6. The Trachtenberg Speed System of Basic Mathematics by Jakow Trachtenberg
  7. Coming of Age in Samoa: A Psychological Study of Primitive Youth for Western Civilisation by Margaret Mead
  8. The Holy Bible
  9. The Last Temptation by Nikos Kazantzakis - a beautiful book on philosophical ruminations and the theological underpinnings of spirituality in christian thought.
  10. Zorba the Greek by Nikos Kazantzakis
  11. Ormakalil Ente Priya Sakhavu by Sarada Teacher
  12. Women, Race & Class by Angela Y. Davis
  13. Witches, Sluts, Feminists: Conjuring the Sex Positive by Kristen J. Sollee
  14. The Second Sex by Simone de Beauvoir
  15. The Rebel by Albert Camus
  16. Ernest Hemingway: The Last Interview
  17. Saint Francis by Nikos Kazantzakis
  18. Justice by Michael Sandel - a lecture series on Ethics, Justice and other topics. Well argued and interactive. Suggested for everyone!
